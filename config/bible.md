- **Liquidity is fragmented across 20+ pools, but ~80% sits in ONE Raydium CLMM/USDC pool.** A large drop in that pool's liquidity (LP removal) typically precedes a dump.

- **Treasury (3PnVBrb4…, role=treasury):** Holds the locked/unsold supply. ANY outflow = potential distribution, unlock, or OTC sale → incoming sell pressure. Highest priority. Treat even a partial-percent move as material.
- **Market maker (ASTyfSima…, role=market_maker):** ~29k SOL, trades constantly. It provides/withdraws liquidity and sets short-term price. Watch the DIRECTION of its net CARDS flow, not the fact that it's active. Net selling = short-term headwind; net buying = tailwind.

- **Main pool (Raydium CLMM/USDC):** Deep liquidity. A sharp liquidity drop = LP pull = bearish; route large trades here to minimize slippage.

## Signal → action playbook
- Treasury or vault OUTFLOW → assume distribution; tighten stops / fade strength.
- Dormant whale WAKES → highest-signal alert; expect a large move; do not be complacent.
- Market-maker net SELLING + price weakness → short-term downside bias.
- Main-pool LIQUIDITY DROP → LP pull often precedes a dump; reduce exposure.
- Several top-25 whales selling into the pool same day → exit signal.
- SOL outflow from a watched wallet → funds being moved (often to a CEX before selling) → early warning.
- CARDS INFLOW / accumulation by a smart wallet → potential bullish positioning.
